Major US Fires, Mapped

Major US Fires from 2003 - 2017 (to Tableau)

Argument

Following the questions begged by the State of Fire in the Union visualizations, it is useful to take a look at a map of major fires in the Unites States. A cursory glance reveals the fires from 2003-2017 cluster on the western side of the country. Within that cluster there is a significant band of fires which stretches from Arizona to Oregon and tapering into Washington, Idaho, and New Mexico. Major fires are unheard of in the Midwest and quite rare on the East Coast and in the South. Additionally, the fires on the East Coast consumed a small area as compared to the western fires. All of the largest fires are located in the western states.

Details

The map plots major wildfires in the United states form 2003-2017. The relative size of the points represents the number of acres burned in the course of the fire. The color gradient used represents the passage of time. The lightest points in yellow represent fires which occurred in 2003 – the oldest points in the dataset. The gradient progresses to red which represents the most recent fires in 2017. The map is derived from the Wikipedia list of Major Fires in the United States and Canada. The Canadian fires were removed for project coherence but may be the point of future interest. Do wildfires in Canada exhibit the same trends as their US counterparts?

Questions

The map indicates that the fires are geographically concentrated in the Western States, particularly California. This reinforces the actuarial data presented by the US Fire Administration. Their visualizations indicate Californians are most likely to be injured by fire. Is there more context this data needs? Are Californians at extreme risk of fire injury or death?
